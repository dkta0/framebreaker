# Research & Inspiration Notes

## Gundam Breaker Mobile (2019–2023)

**The discontinued game we're learning from.** iOS/Android. Developed by Bandai Namco. 
Ran July 2019 – June 5, 2023. Shut down ~2 months before its 4th anniversary.

### What worked
- Cross-series Franken-building: mixing parts from UC, Wing, SEED, 00, IBO, Build Fighters
- Word Tag synergy system — thematic builds rewarded mechanically
- Attribute elemental matrix — soft type-advantage layer
- EX Skill visual flair — every suit's special attacks animated distinctly
- Custom paint/decal system — produced genuine player art
- Weekly content cadence for ~4 years
- Battlogue anime tie-in (ONA, 2021)
- Part break system: enemy parts physically break off as loot

### What failed
- Gacha banners + powercreep — new parts outclassed everything from 2 months prior
- PvP whale gap — ranked play became pay-to-win; one-shot frame-0 meta
- No offline/preservation mode — all player progress lost at EoS
- Technical performance on older devices
- Abrupt shutdown with unresolved story arcs
- Premium cosmetics priced like physical Gunpla kits
- Auto-battle felt passive

### System specs (for reference)
- 8 equipment slots: head, torso, arms, legs, backpack, shield, melee, ranged
- Rarity: 1★–7★ (gacha-gated)
- Squad system: 3 Gunpla (lead + 2 AI support)
- EX Skills: up to 4 equipped simultaneously from any part
- Burst Mode: charged super-state, enhanced stats + movesets
- Pilots: separate gacha pool, passive squad bonuses
- Attributes: Fire, Ice, Wind, Thunder, Light, Dark
- Word Tags: 2 per part, synergy thresholds at 8/10/12 link points
- Business model: free-to-play gacha

## Gundam Breaker 4 (Console/PC, 2024)

GB4 = the course correction. Premium title, no gacha, no ranked PvP. 250+ kits.

### What GB4 got right (relative to GBM)
- Asymmetric arm loadout — left/right arms independent
- Synthesis system: Cannibalize absorbs skills between parts (decouples aesthetics from stats)
- Mastered skills at 5★: EX Skill becomes portable across builds
- No mandatory PvP — Bounty Hunter mode is stakes-free
- Community praised the monetization model compared to GBM
- 250+ kits at launch including Witch from Mercury

### What GB4 still got wrong
- Stage design regressed (inherited GBM's wave arenas, lost GB3 map traversal)
- Combat felt slower than GB3
- Minimal graphics improvement over GB3 (8-year gap)
- Story mode gating frustrates build-first players
- Overall difficulty too low for endgame
- Mastered part displays as holographic silhouette (disliked visually)
- Camera system criticized

## Series DNA (consistent across franchise)

1. **Break → Collect → Build** — the signature mechanic, never absent
2. **Cross-series Franken-building** — entire IP catalog as a parts library
3. **Aesthetic ≠ Stats** — synthesis/merge systems aim to decouple appearance from performance
4. **Loot-driven replayability** — fundamentally a looter; missions replayed for rarer parts
5. **EX Skills as personality** — each unit's specials animate distinctly as love letters to source material
6. **Community as content** — screenshot/diorama culture and build sharing are integral

## What FrameBreaker Is Fixing

| GBM Pain Point | Our Solution |
|----------------|-------------|
| Whale PvP ranked | Bounty Hunt = async, stakes-free, no ladder |
| Gacha powercreep | No gacha — all parts earned in-game |
| P2W cosmetics | Stats only from gameplay; cosmetics priced fairly |
| Passive auto-battle | Manual hack-and-slash (à la GB3/GB4) |
| Abrupt EoS = all progress gone | Open source — community can fork and continue |
| Mastery locked to equipped part | 5★M system: skills portable to any build |
| Wave-arena stage regression | Mixed map types + traversal stages |
